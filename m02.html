Why direct-to-consumer is the most powerful model for new FMCG brands in India today.

What is D2C and why does it matter for NOVI

Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) means you sell directly to your customer -- no distributor, no retailer, no middleman. You own the relationship completely.

In traditional FMCG, a brand like Pedigree sells through distributors who sell to retailers who sell to customers. The brand never speaks directly to the buyer. D2C brands like NOVI bypass all of this.

Every Indian FMCG D2C success story -- Mamaearth, boAt, Lenskart, Wow Skin Science -- was built by owning the customer relationship directly. This is your biggest structural advantage over legacy brands.

The D2C advantage -- why NOVI can beat big brands

  • Customer data -- You know exactly who buys, what they buy, when they buy. Pedigree does not know their end customer. You will.
  • Margins -- Removing middlemen means 30-50% better margins than traditional retail brands at the same price point.
  • Speed -- You can change your product, packaging or messaging in days. A traditional brand takes months to get retailer approval.
  • Story -- D2C brands can tell their story authentically. Customers buy the brand, not just the product.
  • Feedback loop -- Customer comments, reviews and DMs are direct product development intelligence.

Key D2C metrics you must know

These four numbers define whether your D2C business is healthy or dying. Learn them before your first sale.

  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) -- How much you spend to get one customer. If you spend Rs 500 on ads and get 10 customers, CAC is Rs 50.
  • LTV (Lifetime Value) -- Total revenue from one customer over their entire relationship with NOVI. If a customer buys Rs 500 per month for 12 months, LTV is Rs 6,000.
  • LTV:CAC Ratio -- The golden ratio. Must be at least 3:1. If LTV is Rs 6,000 and CAC is Rs 500, ratio is 12:1. Excellent.
  • Gross Margin -- Revenue minus cost of goods. If NOVI sells at Rs 500 and product costs Rs 150, gross margin is 70%. Healthy D2C needs 60%+ gross margin.
Case Study -- boAt
From zero to Rs 3,000 crore in 6 years
boAt launched in 2016 selling audio products directly online. They had no retail presence, no distributor. They used Instagram and Facebook ads to drive traffic to their website. They focused obsessively on LTV -- making customers buy earphones, then headphones, then speakers. By owning the customer relationship they could cross-sell and upsell relentlessly. Today they are India's largest consumer audio brand. NOVI can replicate this playbook in pet care.
NOVI Application
NOVI's D2C strategy
NOVI should sell primarily through its own website and WhatsApp initially. This gives you full customer data, full margins and full control. Once you have 100 loyal customers and understand their behaviour -- then consider marketplaces like Amazon or Flipkart as a secondary channel. Never the other way around.

D2C vs marketplace -- what is right for NOVI right now

Many founders make the mistake of launching on Amazon first. Do not do this. Amazon makes it easy to start but you own nothing -- no customer data, no relationship, and Amazon takes 25-35% commission.

Start D2C. Build your customer base. Learn what they want. Then expand to marketplaces from a position of strength.
