How Marketing Actually Works
The mental models and fundamental truths top strategists use — tailored to NOVI and FMCG D2C brands.
60 min
10-Q quiz
1 assignment

What most people get wrong about marketing

Most people think marketing is about promotion — ads, posts, campaigns, creatives. They're wrong. That's only the visible surface of marketing.

Marketing, at its core, is about one thing: making the right people aware that the right product exists at the right time. Everything else — ads, content, SEO, influencers — is just the mechanism.

The best marketers don't think about campaigns. They think about people. What does this person want, believe, fear, and how does my product fit into their life?

Peter Drucker said: "The aim of marketing is to know and understand the customer so well that the product or service fits them and sells itself." That one sentence contains everything you need to know.

The marketing funnel — and why it's incomplete

The classic funnel: Awareness → Interest → Consideration → Purchase. You've probably seen it. It's also incomplete for modern D2C brands.

Two critical stages are missing — Retention and Advocacy. A customer who buys once is a transaction. A customer who buys repeatedly and tells their friends is a business.

The 4Ps — how D2C brands reinterpret them

The 4Ps — Product, Price, Place, Promotion — were developed in 1960 for traditional retail. D2C brands like NOVI interpret them completely differently.

  • Product — In D2C, product is your biggest marketing asset. NOVI's formulation, packaging, and results are marketing tools.
  • Price — You control pricing completely. Premium price communicates premium pet care brand.
  • Place — For D2C, place is digital — your website, Instagram, WhatsApp. You control the entire customer experience.
  • Promotion — In 2025, promotion means content, community, and targeted paid advertising.

Jobs To Be Done — the most important concept you'll learn

Harvard professor Clayton Christensen developed the Jobs To Be Done (JTBD) framework. Simple idea: people don't buy products. They "hire" products to do a job for them.

Nobody buys a drill because they want a drill. They buy it because they want a hole in the wall. The drill is just the means to that end.

Case Study — Mamaearth
How understanding the "job" built a ₹10,000 crore brand
When Mamaearth launched in 2016, they asked: what job are new parents actually hiring baby care products to do? Not "clean my baby" — but "give me peace of mind that I'm not poisoning my child." That emotional insight shaped everything: toxin-free formulation, transparent ingredients, the brand name itself. They didn't build a better shampoo. They solved a parent's anxiety. That's JTBD.

Applying JTBD to NOVI

What job are pet owners hiring NOVI to do? Surface answer: feed and care for my pet. But dig deeper. What's the emotional job?

Urban Mumbai pet owners see pets as family. They feel anxious about wrong food choices. They want to be seen as responsible, caring pet parents.

NOVI Application
The job NOVI should be hired for
NOVI doesn't sell pet food. NOVI sells confidence — the feeling that you're giving your pet the very best, that you're a great pet parent, that your animal is thriving because of you. Every post, every label, every customer interaction should reinforce this emotional job.

The one truth that never changes

Platforms change. Algorithms change. Ad costs change. But one thing never changes:

People buy from people and brands they trust.

Every post, ad, and customer interaction either builds trust or erodes it. There is no neutral action in marketing. Keep this as your north star.
