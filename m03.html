Who is the Indian urban pet owner in 2025

Before you market NOVI, you must understand exactly who you are marketing to. The Indian pet care market has transformed dramatically in the last decade.

The Indian pet owner in 2025 -- particularly in cities like Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi -- is:

  • 25-40 years old, dual income household
  • Treats their pet as a family member or child substitute
  • Willing to spend significantly on pet health and wellbeing
  • Highly active on Instagram and YouTube for pet content
  • Distrustful of traditional pet food brands after global recalls and ingredient scandals
  • Seeking premium, transparent, India-made alternatives

The India pet care market is growing at 14% annually and is expected to reach Rs 20,000 crore by 2027. Urban pet owners spend an average of Rs 3,000-8,000 per month on their pets. This is your market.

The consumer decision journey for pet food

When a pet owner decides to buy a new pet food brand, they go through a specific journey. Understanding this journey tells you exactly where and how to reach them.

The 5 psychological drivers of pet owner buying behaviour

  • Guilt reduction -- Pet owners who work long hours feel guilty leaving their pets alone. Premium food reduces guilt. "At least I'm feeding them the best."
  • Social signalling -- Premium pet food signals to other pet owners that you are a responsible, caring parent. Seen in pet community groups and Instagram posts.
  • Fear of harm -- Ingredient scares, recalls and health issues drive switching behaviour. A pet owner whose dog had digestive issues will pay any price for the right food.
  • Vet validation -- "My vet recommended it" is the single most powerful purchase driver. Build relationships with vets in Mumbai.
  • Visible results -- Shinier coat, better energy, less shedding. Tangible results drive loyalty more than any marketing claim.
Case Study -- Heads Up For Tails
Building India's premium pet lifestyle brand
Heads Up For Tails started as a pet accessories brand but understood something crucial: urban Indian pet owners do not want pet products. They want to express their love for their pets. HUFT positioned everything as an extension of that love -- premium packaging, beautiful store design, content that celebrated the human-pet bond. Their Instagram is not about products. It is about the joy of pet parenthood. NOVI should study their content strategy carefully.
NOVI Application
Your target customer profile
NOVI's primary customer: Urban Mumbai pet owner, 25-38 years old, dog parent (Labrador, Golden Retriever, Indie breed), dual income, follows pet influencers on Instagram, part of at least one WhatsApp pet community group, has tried 2-3 brands and is unsatisfied, willing to pay Rs 800-2500 for the right product. This is who every NOVI post, ad and message should speak to.
